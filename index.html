About Me

I am a Flatiron Research Fellow in the Biophysical Modeling Group (led by Michael Shelley) at the Flatiron Institute's Center for Computational Biology (also led by Michael Shelley). My research focuses on the applied analysis and geometry of soft, fluid, and active matter. Prior to my current appointment, I studied physics and math at the Georgia Institute of Technology and received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics in 2018 from Brown University, where I was advised by Profs. Thomas Powers (Engineering & Physics) and Robert Pelcovits (Physics). As of Fall 2021, I am on the academic job market. Download my CV.
